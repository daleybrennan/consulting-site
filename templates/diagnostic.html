The Situation

At an EXW of €8.00, your wine reaches the New York shelf at roughly $31 a bottle — and that single number decides which shelf, which list, and which buyer you are talking to.

This diagnostic models every step between your cellar door and the consumer: freight and insurance, US duty and excise, the importer and distributor margins that the three-tier system requires, and the final markups on- and off-premise. It then sets the result against the wines you would actually sit beside in market.

The purpose is simple: to show you, in real numbers, where your price lands today — and where the margin works and where it breaks — before a single case ships.

What The Numbers Say

At the expected shelf of $31.24, your wine sits at the top of the observed band ($25–$34) — credible, but with little room above the comparables that already own the placement. Hold the EXW and the shelf is set; move the EXW and every stop downstream moves with it.

There is a clear question of where the price should sit to win the placement you want, and how to get there without eroding the margin each tier needs. That is the work of the strategy that follows — it is not answered here.
